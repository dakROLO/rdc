# Royal Digital Clarity Website Upgrade Plan

## Goal

Turn RoyalDigitalClarity.com from a polished digital business card into a conversion-focused website that helps small-business owners and middle managers recognize the cost of manual work, understand what RDC delivers, and confidently book a consultation.

## Primary audiences

1. **Small-business owners** who want to get more value from Microsoft 365 without buying another oversized platform.
2. **Middle managers and operations leaders** who spend too much time collecting updates, chasing approvals, rebuilding reports, and maintaining disconnected spreadsheets.

## Core positioning

> Royal Digital Clarity finds where teams are losing time, shows what that time may be costing, and designs the simplest practical solution—often using tools the organization already owns.

## Phase 1 — Technical SEO and crawlability

- Standardize the preferred domain as `https://www.royaldigitalclarity.com/`.
- Add `robots.txt`.
- Add `sitemap.xml`.
- Add Organization and Person JSON-LD structured data.
- Add Open Graph and Twitter metadata.
- Improve the homepage title and meta description.
- Add semantic page structure and accessible form labels.
- Confirm all internal links and canonical references use the same domain.

## Phase 2 — Homepage conversion rebuild

- Rewrite the hero around recovering time lost to manual work.
- Add a quantified professional-experience proof strip.
- Replace the oversized hero logo card with a workflow-cost calculator.
- Add a recognizable “problems we solve” section.
- Split content into paths for small-business owners and middle managers.
- Rename consultation options around outcomes and deliverables.
- Add example transformations and modeled savings scenarios.
- Add an FAQ section.
- Add stronger, repeated booking calls to action.

## Phase 3 — Supporting pages

Create dedicated pages for:

- Microsoft 365 Consulting for Small Business
- Workflow Automation Consulting
- Power Platform Consulting
- Automation for Middle Managers
- Practical AI Adoption
- About Dakota
- Results and Examples
- Frequently Asked Questions

Each page should contain:

- A direct answer to the buyer’s main question.
- Recognizable operational problems.
- Practical examples.
- Clear deliverables.
- Related internal links.
- A consultation call to action.

## Phase 4 — Search and AI-answer content

Publish practical question-led articles, beginning with:

1. What can a small business automate with Microsoft 365?
2. When should Microsoft Lists replace Excel?
3. How can a manager automate weekly status reporting?
4. How much does a manual workflow cost?
5. What is a safe first AI use case for a small business?
6. Do I need premium Power Platform licensing?
7. Can Power Automate route approvals and reminders?
8. What should I automate first?

## Phase 5 — Trust and proof

- Add commercial case studies as RDC engagements are completed.
- Add testimonials with permission.
- Add before-and-after workflow examples.
- Add quantified outcomes only when documentation supports them.
- Clearly distinguish Dakota’s career experience from RDC client results.

## Phase 6 — Measurement

Track:

- Consultation button clicks.
- Workflow calculator usage.
- Booking-page visits.
- Completed bookings where available.
- Traffic by service page.
- Search queries and indexed pages in Google Search Console and Bing Webmaster Tools.

## Visual assets needed

1. **Social sharing card** — 1200 × 630 pixels, using the RDC crown/logo and the message “Recover the hours lost to manual work.”
2. **About image** — optional professional photo of Dakota or an RDC-branded workspace image.
3. **Workflow transformation graphics** — simple before/after diagrams for future service and results pages.

## Messaging guardrails

- Use plain language instead of transformation jargon.
- Lead with the buyer’s operational problem, not the Microsoft product.
- Treat modeled savings as estimates and show the assumptions.
- Present professional experience metrics as Dakota’s experience, not as RDC customer results.
- Avoid claims of guaranteed savings, adoption, or ROI.
